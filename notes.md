# Aspira National Open House Redesign Notes

## Objective

This prototype keeps the required live-page information, including the Aspira logo, event dates, time, phone number, location context, campaign imagery, and an RSVP area. The redesign focuses on increasing RSVP intent by making the value of attending clear before asking visitors to submit the form.

## UX and Conversion Decisions

- Moved the RSVP form into the first viewport so visitors can act immediately.
- Reframed the headline around the emotional job to be done: picturing a next chapter, not simply reading an event announcement.
- Split the date information into two scannable cards to clarify that most residences run May 29-30, while Kensington Place runs May 31-June 1.
- Added "What to expect" content to answer practical questions for adult children and prospective residents.
- Added a lightweight location search preview to reduce friction before RSVP.
- Included phone CTA support for users who prefer to speak with someone.
- Added consent language and a reCAPTCHA production note to reflect the live form's privacy and spam-prevention requirements.

## Visual Direction

- Primary font: Arial, per the brief.
- Palette follows the supplied Aspira colour guidelines: Aspira Green `#00BC70`, Deep Green `#163029`, Mint `#DEF0E5`, and White `#FFFFFF`, with accessible web green variants used for text where needed.
- Imagery uses the supplied Open House campaign assets plus a public Aspira lifestyle image from aspiralife.ca.

## Implementation Notes

This is a static high-fidelity prototype. The form demonstrates the intended interaction pattern and can be wired to HubSpot or another CRM endpoint in production.
